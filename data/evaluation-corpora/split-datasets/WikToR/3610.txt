Huntington is a town in Chittenden County, Vermont, United States. The population was 1,938 at the 2010 census. The town was originally called "New Huntington", but the name was changed to "Huntington" in 1795. It was named for landholders Josiah, Charles and Marmaduke Hunt. Huntington is in southeastern Chittenden County, bordered to the southwest by Addison County and to the east by Washington County. The town is located on the west side of the Green Mountains and is centered on the valley of the Huntington River, a north-flowing tributary of the Winooski River. The 4,083-foot (1,244 m) summit of Camel's Hump is in Huntington's northeast corner, on the town boundary with Duxbury. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town of Huntington has a total area of 38.0 square miles (98.5 km2), of which 38.0 square miles (98.3 km2) is land and 0.1 square miles (0.3 km2), or 0.26%, is water.
