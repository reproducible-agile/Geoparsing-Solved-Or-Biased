Salem is a city in and the county seat of Marion County, Illinois, United States. The population was 7,485 at the 2010 census. Salem is located at 38°38′N 88°57′W (38.6282, -88.9482). According to the 2010 census, Salem has a total area of 7.097 square miles (18.38 km2), of which 6.94 square miles (17.97 km2) (or 97.79%) is land and 0.157 square miles (0.41 km2) (or 2.21%) is water. Salem is home to Miracle Whip salad dressing and three homes on the National Register of Historic Places: the Charles and Naomi Bachmann House, the Badollet House, and the William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home.
