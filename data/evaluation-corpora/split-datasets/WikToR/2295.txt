Gordon is a city in Sheridan County in the state of Nebraska, in the Great Plains region of the United States. Its population was 1,612 at the 2010 census. Gordon was incorporated as a village in 1885 when the railroad was extended to that point. It was named for John Gordon, a pioneer settler. Gordon was incorporated in November 1885. In the Lakota language, Gordon is known as tȟáȟča wakpá otȟúŋwahe, meaning "deer river city". Gordon is located at 42°48′25″N 102°12′14″W (42.806956, -102.203812). According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 0.93 square miles (2.41 km2), all of it land.
