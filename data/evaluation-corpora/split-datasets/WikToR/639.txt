Brandon is a city in Minnehaha County, South Dakota, United States and is the largest suburb of Sioux Falls. Brandon is located five miles east of Sioux Falls. The population was estimated at 9,779 by the United States Census Bureau in July 2014, which was up from 8,785 at the 2010 census. A post office called Brandon has been in operation since 1878. The city took its name from Brandon Township. Brandon is located at 43°35′30″N 96°34′50″W (43.591580, -96.580685), along the Big Sioux River and Split Rock Creek. According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 5.41 square miles (14.01 km2), of which, 5.37 square miles (13.91 km2) is land and 0.04 square miles (0.10 km2) is water. The Great Bear Recreation Park is located 2½ miles to the west of Brandon on Rice Street. The Big Sioux Recreation Area, a state recreation area consisting of a large forest surrounding the park's namesake Big Sioux River, is located west of Robert Bennis Elementary. It features many hiking and biking trails and bridges over the river, as well as camping sites and playgrounds.
