Marion is a town in Union Parish, Louisiana, United States. The population was 765 at the 2010 census, a decrease from 806 at the 2000 census. This population includes the village of Truxno in the northwestern corner of the city limits. It is part of the Monroe Metropolitan Statistical Area. The town was named for Francis Marion, a military leader known as the "Swamp Fox". Marion is located at 32°54′6″N 92°14′36″W (32.901772, -92.243354). According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 3.2 square miles (8.3 km2), of which, 3.2 square miles (8.3 km2) of it is land and 0.31% is water. Residents were assigned to Union Parish Public Schools' Marion High School (K-12) until June 2013, when the school closed down due to a lack of students in the 2012-13 school year. Marion students since the 2013-14 academic season go to the Union Parish High School located in Farmerville, Louisiana.
