Cornwall is a town in Addison County, Vermont, United States. It was founded November 3, 1761. The population was 1,185 at the 2010 census. Cornwall is located in south-central Addison County, in the Champlain Valley. It is bordered by the town of Middlebury (the county seat) to the northeast, Salisbury to the southeast, Whiting to the south, Shoreham to the southwest, Bridport to the west, and Weybridge to the north. Otter Creek forms the town border with Salisbury. Vermont Route 30 passes north-south through Cornwall, while Vermont Route 125 passes east-west through the northern part of the town. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town of Cornwall has a total area of 28.6 square miles (74.1 km2), of which 28.5 square miles (73.7 km2) is land and 0.15 square miles (0.4 km2), or 0.57%, is water. Cornwall public school is the kindergarten-sixth grade Bingham Memorial School. Middle school and high school students who live in Cornwall attend Middlebury Union High School in Middlebury, Vermont.
