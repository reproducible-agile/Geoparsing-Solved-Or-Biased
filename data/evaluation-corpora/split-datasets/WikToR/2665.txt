Philadelphia is a town in Jefferson County, New York, United States. The population was 1,947 at the 2010 census. The Town of Philadelphia contains a village called Philadelphia. Both town and village are in the northeast part of Jefferson County. The town was first settled around 1804. The town was formed in 1821 from part of the Town of Le Ray. In 1872, the community of Philadelphia set itself off from the town by incorporating as a village. In 1959, the Indian River Central High School (New York) opened, serving students from the towns of Philadelphia, Theresa, Antwerp, Leray, Pamelia, the villages of Philadelphia, Theresa, Antwerp, Evans Mills, the hamlet of Calcium and the federal military installation of Fort Drum. The Sterlingville Archeological District was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1995.
