Shamsabad is a town and a nagar panchayat in Farrukhabad district in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. As of 2001 India census, Shamsabad had a population of 23,584. Males constitute 53% of the population and females 47%. Shamsabad has an average literacy rate of 43%, lower than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 50%, and female literacy is 36%. In Shamsabad, 18% of the population is under 6 years of age. Mr. Vijay Gupta(2012) is the chairman of Shamsabad. Mr. Mukesh Rajput, is the member of parliament. Shamsabad is situated latitude 27.32 longitude 79.28 at a distance of 22 km to the west of Farrukhabad. Most probably the river Ganges used to flow beside its northern boundary. A mohalla, locality called the Ghatiapur crossing point, is reminiscent of this. The Ganges is about 4 km away now.
