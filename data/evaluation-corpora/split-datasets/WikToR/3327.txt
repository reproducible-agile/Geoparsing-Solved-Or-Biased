Paradise is a census-designated place that is the southernmost community in Mono County, California. Located in the southwest part of the county, Paradise functions as a bedroom community for workers in Bishop (15 miles (24 km) to the south) and Mammoth Lakes, and as a home for retirees. Geographically, it sits near the bottom of the Sherwin Grade, which divides Round Valley in Inyo County from Long Valley in Mono County. Neighboring communities include Wheeler Crest to the west, Swall Meadows and Pinon Ranch to the north. The population was 153 at the 2010 census.
