Abbotsford is a city in Clark (mostly) and Marathon counties in the U.S. state of Wisconsin. It is part of the Wausau, Wisconsin Metropolitan Statistical Area. The population was 2,310 at the 2010 census. Of this, 1,616 were in Clark County, and 694 were in Marathon County. Abbotsford is nicknamed "Wisconsin's First City" due to its alphabetical place on a list of Wisconsin cities. In 1950, Abbotsford had a population of 1,013. The community is named for Edwin H. Abbott, of the Wisconsin Central Railway, and was incorporated in 1965. Abbotsford is located at 44°56′48″N 90°19′6″W (44.946716, -90.318333). According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 2.71 square miles (7.02 km2), all of it land. In 2010 Abbotsford had a population of 2,310. The racial and ethnic composition was 73.7% non-Hispanic white, 0.1% black or African American, 0.3% Native American, 0.7% Asian, 0.5% reporting two or more races and 25.0% Hispanic or Latino. Abbotsford Public Schools are part of the Abbotsford School District. The district has one elementary school, one middle/senior high school and one alternative high school and education center.
