Madison is a city in Greenwood County, Kansas, United States, along the Verdigris River. As of the 2010 census, the city population was 701. Madison was originally founded in 1872 northwest of the present town. The town was relocated in 1879 to its present site when the Atchison, Topeka & Santa Fe Railroad was built through the neighborhood. Madison took its name from Madison Township. Madison is located at 38°7′59″N 96°8′14″W (38.133014, -96.137252). According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 0.61 square miles (1.58 km2), all of it land. The climate in this area is characterized by hot, humid summers and generally mild to cool winters. According to the Köppen Climate Classification system, Madison has a humid subtropical climate, abbreviated "Cfa" on climate maps.
