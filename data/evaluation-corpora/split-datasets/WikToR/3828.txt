Valladolid is a fourth class municipality in the province of Negros Occidental, Philippines. At the 2010 census, it had a population of 36,416 people. Known as the "Food Basket of Negros Occidental," the municipality celebrates its fiesta every 28 February. Valladolid Municipality is located on the south-western coast of the Province of Negros Occidental. It is approximately 31 kilometres (19 mi) south of Bacolod City, a 35-minute drive by private car or 45 minutes by public utility vehicles via the main highway. Valladolid is bounded on the north by the town of Pulupandan, on the south by the municipality of San Enrique, on the west by the Guimaras Strait and on the east by the city of La Carlota. The global location of Municipality of Valladolid is 10 degrees, 27 minutes 54 seconds - north and 122 degrees 49 minutes 33.6 seconds - east with Our Lady of Guadalupe Church as the benchmark. Valladolid has two pronounced seasons, wet and dry. The rainy season starts from May to January of the following year with heavy rains occurring during the months of August and September. Dry season starts from the month of February until the last week of April.
