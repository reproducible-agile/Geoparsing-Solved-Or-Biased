The Department of Health (DHSSPS) in Northern Ireland has announced that a child aged two has died after falling victim to the H1N1 swine flu virus. The toddler had underlying health problems. The death brings the total number of people from Northern Ireland that have died as a result of having the disease to 17, also including one person who was in Spain and one person who was in England at the times of their deaths. Michael McGimpsey, health minister of Northern Ireland, expressed his sympathy. "Sadly, we have been notified of the death of a two-year-old child who was confirmed as having swine flu," he stated. "The child had underlying medical conditions. I would like to express my deepest sympathy to the family at this very sad time for them."
